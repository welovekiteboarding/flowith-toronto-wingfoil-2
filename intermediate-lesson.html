What to Expect in Your Intermediate Lesson

The Intermediate Lesson is for wingfoilers who have successfully completed our Beginner Lesson (or have equivalent experience) and are achieving consistent short flights on the foil. This 2-hour session focuses on refining your technique for longer, more controlled foiling rides, improving your ability to manage height, and introducing basic transitions such as carving turns (both on and off the foil) and the initial steps towards foiling tacks and jibes.

On Lake Ontario, intermediate skills allow you to handle more varied conditions with confidence. You'll learn to adapt your stance and foil control to manage small chop, maintain speed through lulls, and start to link maneuvers. We'll continue to emphasize safety and may introduce slightly more responsive equipment if appropriate for your progress.

Lesson Structure

Skill Review & Goal Setting (20 minutes)

  • Brief review of current foiling ability (water starts, short flights, basic control).
  • Discussion of specific goals for the session (e.g., longer flights, consistent height, starting turns).
  • Introduction to the theory and mechanics of basic transitions.
  • Advanced safety review related to transitions and longer rides.

Sustained Foiling & Height Control (40 minutes)

  • Techniques for achieving longer, more stable flights on the foil.
  • Practicing consistent height control (avoiding porpoising or breaching).
  • Drills for fore-aft and lateral balance adjustments while foiling.
  • Improving upwind and downwind riding angles on the foil.
  • Pumping techniques to maintain foil lift in lighter winds or lulls.

Introduction to Transitions (50 minutes)

  • Learning to initiate carving turns (both heel-side and toe-side) while on the foil.
  • Practicing "touch-down" turns (coming off foil, turning, and re-launching).
  • Introduction to footwork and wing handling for foiling tacks (upwind turns on foil).
  • Introduction to footwork and wing handling for foiling jibes (downwind turns on foil).
  • Focus on maintaining momentum and balance during transitional phases.

Debrief & Path to Advanced (10 minutes)

  • Review of progress on sustained flight and transitions.
  • Personalized feedback and drills for continued practice.
  • Discussion of common challenges in learning transitions and how to overcome them.
  • Guidance on what to focus on next, potentially leading to our Advanced Lesson.

Prerequisites

To benefit most from the Intermediate Lesson, you should have completed our Beginner Lesson or have equivalent experience, and be able to:

  • Consistently get up on the foil and achieve short to medium length rides.
  • Perform basic water starts with good wing and board control.
  • Maintain basic directional control while on foil.
  • Be comfortable recovering your board and wing after falls in deeper water.

What's Included

  • All necessary equipment, potentially including slightly more advanced gear suited for intermediate progression (wing, board, foil, leash, helmet).
  • Wetsuit and personal flotation device (PFD) / Impact Vest.
  • Professional instruction with experienced coach focusing on intermediate skills and transitions.
  • Personalized feedback and video analysis (if conditions/time permit).

Unlock Fluid Riding

The Intermediate Lesson is where your wingfoiling truly starts to flow. Mastering sustained flights and basic transitions opens up the ability to explore more of Toronto's waters, link turns, and ride with greater confidence and style. This stage is incredibly rewarding as you begin to feel more connected with your gear and the elements.

Ready for Advanced Techniques?

Once you're comfortable with sustained foiling and are making progress on basic transitions, you might be ready to explore more advanced maneuvers. Our Advanced Lesson focuses on mastering foiling tacks and jibes, toeside riding, jumps, and more.
