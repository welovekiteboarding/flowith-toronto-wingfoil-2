What to Expect in Your Beginner Lesson

The Beginner Lesson builds on the foundation established in the Discovery Lesson or equivalent prior experience. During this 2-hour session, you'll refine your wing handling skills, improve board control, and work toward your first flights on the foil. This is typically where students experience the unique sensation of foiling for the first time. We will revisit land-based wing control and safety procedures before moving back onto the water with the board and wing to focus on standing starts, gaining speed towards foiling, and achieving short periods lifted by the foil. You'll be using appropriate beginner foil equipment.

In Toronto's conditions, we'll select a location that offers a balance between slightly deeper water (necessary for foiling) and manageable conditions. This might mean moving just beyond the shallowest areas used in Discovery lessons, into waters where the foil has room to operate. Your instructor will guide you on managing small chop if present, a common condition on Lake Ontario, and teach you techniques to get the board planing and the foil lifting.

Lesson Structure

Review & Warm-up (20 minutes)

  • Quick review of skills learned in Discovery Lesson (wing handling, board balance)
  • Discussion of goals for the session, focusing on water starts and first foil attempts
  • Equipment setup (including foil attachment) and enhanced safety check for foiling
  • Wing handling refresher on land, focusing on power generation for water starts

Water Starts & Board Control (40 minutes)

  • Practicing efficient water starts: getting from a kneeling or sitting position to standing on the board while managing the wing
  • Improved board positioning and balance for stability during starts
  • Techniques for generating board speed using the wing (sheeting, pumping)
  • Maintaining directional control and basic turns while on the board (pre-foiling)
  • Body positioning for preparing to foil – subtle weight shifts and pressure control

First Foiling Attempts (50 minutes)

  • Understanding how to initiate the foil's lift through speed and board angle
  • Practicing weight distribution for stable foiling – fore-aft and side-to-side balance
  • Experiencing first short flights on the foil ("touch and go's")
  • Learning to control lift and descent smoothly
  • Fall recovery techniques specific to foiling and restarting attempts
  • Maintaining balance during brief moments of flight

Debrief & Next Steps (10 minutes)

  • Review of progress and achievements, celebrating first flights!
  • Personalized feedback on water start and foiling technique
  • Recommendations for continued improvement and practice
  • Discussion of Intermediate Lesson progression or repeating Beginner Lesson if needed

Skills You'll Develop

  • Advanced wing control for power generation
  • Consistent and efficient water starts
  • Proper body positioning for initiating foiling
  • Techniques to initiate foil lift
  • Maintaining balance during initial foil flights
  • Quick recovery and restart techniques after falls

Prerequisites

To get the most out of the Beginner Lesson, you should have completed a Discovery Lesson or have equivalent experience. You should be comfortable with basic wing handling and board skills, including:

  • Basic wing control (holding, sheeting in/out, steering the wing)
  • Ability to body drag with the wing in shallow water
  • Comfortable getting on the board while holding the wing and maintaining balance in shallow water
  • Understanding of basic safety procedures and wind principles

What's Included

  • All equipment specifically chosen for beginner foilers (wing, board with foil, leash, helmet)
  • Wetsuit and personal flotation device (PFD) / Impact Vest
  • Professional instruction with experienced coach focusing on foiling progression
  • Comprehensive safety supervision and guidance in slightly deeper water

The Foiling Experience: First Flights

Experiencing your first "flights" on the foil is an unforgettable sensation. As you build speed and the foil begins to lift, you'll feel the board rise above the water's surface, creating an incredibly smooth and silent gliding experience. This moment of "flying" above the water is what makes wingfoiling so unique and addictive. Our instructors will be right there with you, providing tips and support to help you achieve these initial lifts.

Don't worry if your first flights are brief—this is normal! Each attempt builds muscle memory and understanding of the foil's behavior. By the end of this lesson, most students achieve several short flights and begin to get a feel for the balance and control required for sustained foiling. We emphasize a gradual approach, ensuring you feel confident and safe at each step.

Next Steps After This Lesson

After the Beginner Lesson, most students continue to the Intermediate Lesson to refine their foiling skills, work on longer flights, improve height control, and begin learning basic transitions. Some students may benefit from an additional Beginner Lesson to solidify their foiling foundations and gain more confidence before moving to more advanced techniques. Your instructor will provide personalized recommendations.
