What to Expect in Your First Lesson

Our Discovery Lesson is designed for first-time wingfoilers with no prior experience. This 2-hour session introduces you to the fundamentals of wingfoiling in a safe, controlled environment. You'll learn the basics of wing handling, board control, and safety protocols that will form the foundation for your wingfoiling journey. We begin with theory and land-based practice, focusing on understanding wind principles and controlling the wing. We then move to shallow water to practice body dragging and getting comfortable with the board before attempting to stand and move with the wing and board together.

In Toronto, we often utilize locations like Cherry Beach or Woodbine Beach for Discovery Lessons, as these spots can offer shallow, calm water areas depending on wind direction. This makes it easier to stand, manage the equipment, and practice without the challenge of deep water or significant waves. Your instructor will be with you every step of the way, ensuring you feel comfortable and confident. We use beginner-friendly equipment, often starting with a large, stable board without the foil attached.

Lesson Structure

Introduction & Safety Briefing (30 minutes)

  • Welcome and overview of wingfoiling as a sport
  • Detailed introduction to equipment: wing, board (without foil initially for safety), leash, PFD, helmet
  • Comprehensive safety protocols: understanding wind, water conditions, potential hazards, and self-rescue basics
  • Weather and water conditions assessment for the specific Toronto location
  • Basic terminology and communication signals used during the lesson

Wing Handling on Land (20 minutes)

  • Proper wing holding technique and setup (inflation, leash attachment)
  • Wing control and manipulation: sheeting in/out, steering, flipping the wing
  • Understanding wind direction, the "wind window," and power zones
  • Simulating sailing maneuvers on land to get a feel for the wing's power
  • Practicing emergency depower of the wing

Water Practice - Shallow Water Focus (60 minutes)

  • Entering the water safely with equipment (wing and board)
  • Practicing body dragging with the wing: using the wing to pull you through the water without the board
  • Learning to get on the board (large, stable beginner board) while controlling the wing in shallow water
  • Developing basic board balance and body positioning (kneeling, then standing)
  • First attempts at moving a short distance on the board using wing power
  • Recovery techniques if you fall off the board or lose the wing

Debrief & Next Steps (10 minutes)

  • Review of skills learned and progress made during the session
  • Personalized feedback, tips for improvement, and what to practice
  • Discussion of next steps: moving to the Beginner Lesson to work towards foiling
  • Recommendations for future lessons and how to continue your wingfoiling journey

Skills You'll Learn

  • Proper wing handling and control techniques (on land and water)
  • Understanding wind direction, power zones, and basic aerodynamics
  • Basic board balance, positioning, and getting onto the board
  • Essential water safety, self-rescue techniques, and hazard awareness
  • Body dragging with the wing for board recovery and control
  • Basic equipment care, setup, and handling

What's Included

  • All necessary equipment (beginner-friendly wing, large stable board, leash, helmet)
  • Wetsuit and personal flotation device (PFD) / Impact Vest
  • Professional instruction with experienced and certified coach
  • Comprehensive safety briefing and ongoing supervision

What to Bring for Your Lesson

  • Swimsuit (to wear under the wetsuit)
  • Towel
  • Waterproof Sunscreen
  • Water bottle (stay hydrated!)
  • Sunglasses (optional, with a retainer strap)
  • A positive attitude and willingness to learn something new and exciting!

Next Steps After This Lesson

After completing the Discovery Lesson, most students progress to the Beginner Lesson where you'll build on these foundations, focus on water starts, and work toward your first flights on the foil. Some students may benefit from a second Discovery Lesson to further solidify the fundamentals of wing and board control before moving to the next level. Your instructor will provide personalized advice based on your progress.
